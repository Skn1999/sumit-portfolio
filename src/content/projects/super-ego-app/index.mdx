## Overview

Have you ever opened your phone to check the weather and, twenty minutes later, found yourself deep in Instagram with no memory of the detour? That gap between what we *intend* to do and what we *actually* do is what this project is about.

YOU (SuperEgo) is an assistant that doesn't live inside an app. It lives inside the operating system itself, watching the quiet signals we don't notice, for example how fast we switch between apps, how late we're still scrolling. It pairs those signals with a gentle understanding of how emotionally self-aware each person is, then nudges accordingly (sometimes with friction, sometimes with a reflective question that stops you mid-scroll).

> _I led the research synthesis, behavioral framing, system logic, and interaction design, and built the hi-fi prototype._

## What I designed

### The big idea: your phone's quiet conscience

Think of Freud's SuperEgo concept. Not as a strict parent, but as a thoughtful inner voice that notices when you're on autopilot. That's YOU. It doesn't live in an app you have to remember to open. It lives at the OS level, present in the background, watching patterns (never content), and stepping in at the moments that matter.

<ProjectImageAsset src="super-ego-app/integration.jpg" alt="Screenshot: System overview - data inputs, emotional awareness, interventions" />

### Onboarding that feels like a conversation

Most apps dump you into a settings screen. YOU starts with a conversation. Pick a goal, but only one. Try to pick three? YOU gently explains that focus works better than ambition. Not sure? Tap "Help me decide" and YOU observes your patterns for a week before suggesting anything.

Then choose your role for the system â€” how much do you want it to speak up? At the end, it summarizes everything: your goal, what YOU will watch for, how it'll intervene, and exactly how to change any of it.

<ProjectImageAsset src="super-ego-app/onboarding.jpg" alt="Screenshot: Goal selection and 'Help me decide' flow" />

### Getting to know your emotional landscape

Inspired by the **LEAS framework**, I created short "Help me understand you" exercises. You read a social scenario, answer how you'd feel and how the other person might feel, then rate a few statements about how easily you process emotions.

Based on responses, YOU calibrates everything. <br/> 
**High emotional awareness â†’ nuanced, layered prompts**. <br/>
**Still building that vocabulary â†’ simpler, warmer ones.**

<ProjectImageAsset src="super-ego-app/emotional-awareness-LEAS.jpg" alt="Screenshot: Emotional awareness vignette - 'Help me understand you'" />

### When the system should step back

YOU isn't a therapist. If Phil's stress stays high for days, it doesn't double down â€” it reframes: *"Maybe the goal right now isn't to lower stress. It's to understand it."*. And gently asking, *"Would it help to talk to someone who isn't an app?"*

Users can always adjust their level, pause goals without losing progress, or change direction.
